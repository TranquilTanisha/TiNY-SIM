Quick Start

What is TiNY SIM?

TiNY Steganography based Information Manipulation or TiNY SIM for short is an easy to use tool providing users a way to send messages or data through the means of images in a secure and safe manner. It uses the principles of steganography which is the technique of hiding secret data within a file or in order to avoid detection.

What is Steganography?

Steganography is the practice of concealing information within other information in a way that is not apparent to the casual observer. Its goal is to hide the existence of the information, rather than to hide the content itself.
It can be used to hide information in a variety of media, including images, audio files, and text documents. The most common method involves modifying the least significant bits of a digital file. By altering these bits, it is possible to embed information in the file without significantly changing the appearance or sound of the file.

Why do we need such a tool?

In an evergrowing world of technology, privacy and security has become a major concern of many. How private are the messages, videos or images you share over the internet? To tackle this problem, we planned to devise a method which allows a user to send data to another user without revealing any information about the nature of the information shared.

Guide

Homepage

The homepage provides the user with 2 options, whether they would like to encode data into an image or if they would rather retrieve the encoded data from an image (decode).

To go to the desired page, just click on the respective side of the page.

Encode

On the encode page, users shall be asked to provide the website with:
∙ The image they wish to encode the message into
∙ The message itself
∙ The new image's filename
Clicking on "Encode And Download" will automatically download the new image with the encoded message onto the system. The user will also get a Super-Key along with the image which will be required at the time of decoding.

Decode

On the decode page, users shall be asked to provide the website with:
∙ The image they wish to decode the message from
∙ The super-key for the corresponding image
Clicking on "Decode" will display the message which was stored in the image onto the site only if the super-key matches with the image.
